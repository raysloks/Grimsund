Actions

Every turn you may take one action and one half action.

You may freely exchange one action for two half actions and vice versa.

Melee Attack

Make a Melee Attack contest versus your target's Melee Defense.

This contest is special, in that you rolling a natural 20 means you automatically win, and you rolling a natural 1 means you automatically lose.

If you win, you deal your weapon's damage dice to the target.

Ranged Attack

Making a ranged attack provokes attacks of opportunity.

Move

Move 6 paces.

Recover

Regain 1d6 + Constitution hit points, but you need to make a Constitution save or gain a stack of Exhaustion.

Half Actions

Sprint

Move 1d6 paces.

Reactions

Attack of Opportunity

Momentum

When you win a contest versus an opponent, you gain a stack of momentum.

Each stack of momentum grants you a +2 bonus to any combat related rolls.

You can normally have up to 3 stacks of momentum.

If you lose a contest, you lose all stacks of momentum.

Flanking

If there is only one enemy adjacent to you, you are marking that enemy.

If a marked enemy makes a melee attack against someone other than you or is the target of a melee attack, you may make an attack opportunity against them.

Damage

An attack that hits will always deal at least 1 damage, even if it is reduced by armor.

If an attack rolls the maximum value on any of its damage dice, it also inflicts a minor wound.

If an attack reduces the target to 0 hit points, it inflicts a major wound. If the attack would already inflict a wound, it inflicts a fatal wound.

An attack can only inflict a single wound.

Wounds

Minor wounds will never incur permanent penalties.

Major wounds may incur permanent penalties, and have a low chance of being fatal.

Fatal wounds are almost always, as the name suggests, fatal.
