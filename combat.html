Basics

Strike

Make a Melee Offense test versus the target's Melee Defense.

This test is special. If you roll a natural 20 you automatically succeed. If you roll a natural 1 you automatically fail.

If you succeed, your attack hits and you deal your weapon's damage dice to the target.

Shot

Make a Ranged Offense test versus your target's Ranged Defense plus 1 per 10 paces the target is further than your weapon's mid range.

This test is special. If you roll a natural 20 you automatically succeed. If you roll a natural 1 you automatically fail.

If you succeed, your attack hits and you deal your weapon's damage dice to the target.

Movement

Most movement in combat occurs through the actions Charge, Stride, and Sprint.

Turns

At the start of a time-critical encounter, every player rolls a d20 to use as their Initiative. This value may be modified by various abilities, but it is always a value between 1 and 20.

Regardless, record the result as your Initiative. You could do this via sticky notes or some sort of dry-erase stuff.

The players begin taking turns, starting at the highest Initiative.

Actions

Every turn you may take one action.

Note that the actions listed here are not an exhaustive list.

Melee Attack

Action

Make a Strike against an adjacent target.

Allows Opportunity Attacks, except from the target.

Your target also allows Opportunity Attacks, except from you.

Going back and forth between having this be a contest, or having it as a test.

Ranged Attack

Action

Allows Opportunity Attacks.

Make a Shot against a target in range.

Requires more research, refining, and testing.

Stride

Action

Move up to 5 paces.

Sprint

Action

Move up to 10 paces.

Make a DC 16 Constitution save or gain a level of exhaustion.

Allows Opportunity Attacks from anyone you move away from or pass on the way.

Charge

Action

Move up to 5 paces towards a target and make a Strike against it.

Allows Opportunity Attacks from anyone you move away from or pass on the way.

Recover

Action

Regain 3 hit points. Make a DC 12 Constitution save or gain a level of exhaustion.

Hold

Action

Put your turn on hold. After another player's turn you may interject, resuming your turn then.

Holding your turn does not change your Initiative.

You must finish your held turn before you can take your next turn.

Hold refunds the action spent on it when you resume your turn.

Reactions

Momentum

When you hit an attack, you gain a stack of momentum.

Each stack of momentum grants you a +2 bonus to any combat related rolls.

You can normally have up to 3 stacks of momentum.

If you miss an attack or get hit by an attack, you lose all stacks of momentum.

Flanking

If there is only one enemy adjacent to you, you are marking that enemy.

If a marked enemy "allows Opportunity Attacks" from you, you may make an Opportunity Attack against them.

Opportunity Attacks

An Opportunity Attack, also known as an attack of opportunity, is for most purposes a free Strike that may be made under a few circumstances.

Considering adding a penalty to opportunity attacks made after the first in a round. Perhaps even all opportunity attacks.

Damage

A hit will always deal at least 1 nonlethal damage, even if it is reduced to 0 or below by Armor or such.

If the attacker has 3 or more stacks of momentum after hitting, it also inflicts a minor wound.

If a hit reduces the target to 0 hit points, or they were already at 0 hit points, it inflicts a major wound. If the hit would already inflict a wound, it inflicts a fatal wound.

A hit can only inflict a single wound.

Nonlethal

Can never inflict fatal wounds and always uses the unarmed wounds table.

Wounds

Minor wounds will never incur permanent penalties.

Major wounds may incur permanent penalties, and have a low chance of being fatal if not treated.

Fatal wounds are almost always, as the name suggests, fatal.

Conditions

Stunned

May not take any actions or reactions.

Strikes against stunned targets have advantage.

Prone

Movement limited to one pace.

Disadvantage on Agility tests.

Disadvantage on Strikes and Shots, unless the weapon has the 'prone' tag.

Strikes against prone targets have advantage.

Shots against prone targets have disadvantage.

May use an action to stand up, allowing Opportunity Attacks.
