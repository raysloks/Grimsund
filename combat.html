Actions

Every turn you may take one action.

Melee Attack

Make a Melee Attack test versus your target's Melee Defense.

This test is special. If you roll a natural 20 you automatically win. If you roll a natural 1 you automatically lose.

If you win, you deal your weapon's damage dice to the target.

Going back and forth between having this be a contest, or having it as a test.

Ranged Attack

Make a Ranged Attack test versus your target's Ranged Defense plus 1 per 10 paces the target is further than your weapon's mid range.

Making a ranged attack provokes attacks of opportunity.

Requires more research, refining, and testing.

Move

Move up to 5 paces.

Recover

Regain 1d6 + Constitution hit points, but you need to make a DC 16 Constitution save or gain a level of Exhaustion.

Sprint

Move up to 1d6 + 5 paces.

Make a DC 16 Constitution save or gain a level of exhaustion.

May be a bit slow to make two separate rolls when recovering or sprinting. Testing required.

Charge

Move up to 5 paces towards a target and make a melee attack against it.

Momentum

When you hit an attack or an attack against you misses, you gain a stack of momentum.

Each stack of momentum grants you a +2 bonus to any combat related rolls.

You can normally have up to 3 stacks of momentum.

If you miss an attack or get hit by an attack, you lose all stacks of momentum.

Attacks of Opportunity

An attack of opportunity is for most purposes a free melee attack that may be made under a few circumstances.

Considering adding a penalty to attacks of opportunity made after the first in a round. Perhaps even all attacks of opportunity.

Flanking

If there is only one enemy adjacent to you, you are marking that enemy.

If a marked enemy makes a melee attack against someone other than you; is the target of a melee attack; or uses the Sprint or Charge action, you may make an attack of opportunity against them.

Damage

An attack that hits will always deal at least 1 damage, even if it is reduced by armor.

If an attack rolls the maximum value on any of its damage dice, it also inflicts a minor wound.

If an attack reduces the target to 0 hit points, it inflicts a major wound. If the attack would already inflict a wound, it inflicts a fatal wound.

An attack can only inflict a single wound.

The rule for how minor wounds occur is the one part I am unsure about. It gives daggers and such a larger chance to inflict a minor wound, which I guess makes sense. But I'm not sure that's what I want.

Wounds

Minor wounds will never incur permanent penalties.

Major wounds may incur permanent penalties, and have a low chance of being fatal if not treated.

Fatal wounds are almost always, as the name suggests, fatal.
